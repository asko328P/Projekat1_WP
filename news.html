OpenStreetMap

OpenStreetMap is a community project to create a free (as in freedom, not as in beer) editable map. Started in 2004, this project has amassed users from all over the world, as many as 7,000,000 unique users. This trend is exponentionally rising as more and more people and bussinesses realize the importance of free maps. With many 'big players' starting to contribute and rely on them, such as Facebook, Apple, Amazon and others.

The OpenStreetMap Foundation

The OpenStreetMap Foundation (or short as FSF) is a non-profit organization which owns and maintains all of the data that the OSM is made of. They are responsible for the creating the initiative for providing free geographic data to anyone. That even includes comercial services. They are supporting but not controlling the OSM project. The OSM logo

Quality? Is it good as paid alternatives?

The first speculation or doubts someone might have would probably sound something like: Anybody can put everything they want, quality would be compromised. And they have every right to be concerned, but thankfuly the OSM community absolutely takes ground truth as their top priority. Every change that might be incorrect, a peer OSM-mapper can quickly and easily correct. This also allows for new information about roads, like construction zones, new roads etc. to be rapidly added, much faster than any comercial alternative out there.
